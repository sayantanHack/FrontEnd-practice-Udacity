Eagle

Eagles are some of the largest birds. They are at the top of the food chain, with some species feeding on big prey like monkeys and sloths.

  • Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus
  • Average Length: 70-84 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 17 years
  • Habitat: Northwest coast of North America

Eagle is the common name for many large birds of prey of the family Accipitridae. Eagles belong to several groups of genera, not all of which are closely related. Most of the 60 species of eagle are from Eurasia and Africa.
Eagles normally build their nests, called eyries, in tall trees or on high cliffs. Many species lay two eggs, but the older, larger chick frequently kills its younger sibling once it has hatched.
